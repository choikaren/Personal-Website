Today I Will Not Stress Over The Things I Cannot Control
2018
Mural in SITHS
By Karen Choi, Kristy Lee, Jessica Liu
This mural aims to deconstruct the reasons of stress at school.
Spanning over two walls, the mural's six components are connected with a running heart beat. Within each side of the wall, we identified factors that causes stress and destress.
The heart leads into 'Guns, Germs, and Steel' - a book read in our mandatory AP World History class. Known to be a dense and often difficult to read, it was a factor causing stress at our school. Then, it continues into a figure embodying anxiety. It grasps onto spider lilies, also known as 'flowers of death'. Here,the heartbeat wraps around, similar to the way anxiety can deteriorate and constrict our emotional wellbeing. The next facet we identified that aligns with stress is unhealthy foods. We are what we eat and the foods we put into our bodies very well affects the way we feel and hold ourselves.
Directly opposite of and juxtaposing the unhealthy foods are the healthy foods. We believe that fueling ourselves with good foods has immensely positive impacts. When we eat clean, we think and feel better. The next aspect of managing stress is staying active and involved with the community. We painted polaroid-like frames of good memories associated with sports, which allow us to exercise and take good care of not only our physical but also mental health. Having sports provides us with a team that can become our second family, and allows us to develop meaningful relationships with others that have the same interests. Lastly, having a hobby or creative outlet is incredibly important. Especially as now, more than ever, finding a way to express ourselves gives us a way to reflect and connect with the world around us. And, having something to dedicate our time to such as music can also be soothing and calming. Hidden in the music notes is the start of the song 'You've Got a Friend In Me' featured in Toy Story.
